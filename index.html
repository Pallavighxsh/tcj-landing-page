Standard Operating Procedure (SOP)

Introduction: This SOP outlines PHI Learning’s journal processes to ensure academic integrity and transparency.

Scope: Applies to all stakeholders—authors, editors, reviewers, and publication staff.

Editorial Structure: Roles include Editor-in-Chief (final decisions), Editorial Board (quality oversight), Managing Editor (workflow), Reviewers (evaluation), and Production Team (publishing).

Submission Guidelines: Manuscripts must follow journal formatting and pass plagiarism checks (<10%). Disclosure of conflicts and funding is required.

Peer Review: Double-blind review ensures fairness. Submissions are assessed for quality, originality, and relevance. Final decisions include: accept, revise, or reject.

Ethical Standards: Follows COPE guidelines. Plagiarism and data fabrication are prohibited. Confidentiality and permissions are mandatory.

Publication: Accepted work undergoes editing, proofing, and DOI assignment. Published quarterly in print and online.

Post-Publication: Corrections, retractions, and open dialogue through Letters to the Editor are supported.

Marketing: Promoted via conferences, social media, and open-access repositories.

6 Steps to Get Published
‘The Continuum’, PHI Learning’s Academic Journal

1

Submit your research papers to us via email for initial assessment by our editorial team.

2

Senior editors evaluate the submission for relevance, originality, and adherence to journal guidelines.

3

Manuscripts undergo plagiarism screening and a double-blind peer review where experts assess research depth and novelty.

4

Authors receive feedback, make revisions, and resubmit for final evaluation.

5

Upon approval, the manuscript moves to copyediting, formatting, and DOI assignment before publication.

6

Your research is published online and in print, indexed for academic accessibility, and promoted through PHI Learning’s networks.
